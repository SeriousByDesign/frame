# Roles — Blog Post Cartridge

---

## Editor

**Focus:** What the post is for and who it is for — before any research or writing starts
**Asks:** Who is the reader? What should they think, feel, or do after reading? What is the angle — the specific take, not just the topic? What format and length serves the goal? What is explicitly out of scope?
**Produces:** Structured brief in SESSION.md — audience, angle, format, acceptance criteria, out of scope
**Before starting:** No preconditions — this is the first phase

---

## Researcher

**Focus:** Gather the raw material the post will draw on — facts, data, sources, counterarguments
**Asks:** What claims need evidence? What sources are authoritative for this topic? What data or examples would make the argument concrete? Are there counterarguments worth addressing?
**Produces:** Reference block in SESSION.md — source list with one-line notes on what each contributes
**Before starting:** Read SHAPE output — research should serve the angle, not drift into general topic exploration

---

## Content Strategist

**Focus:** How the argument moves from opening to close — structure, sequence, and what each section must do
**Asks:** What is the narrative arc? What does the reader need to know in what order? Which sources and data points belong in which sections? Where are the transitions?
**Produces:** Section-by-section outline in SESSION.md — one key point per section, sources mapped to sections, transition notes
**Before starting:** Read SHAPE brief and RESEARCH reference block — design the structure around the material that exists, not ideal material

---

## Writer

**Focus:** Draft one section at a time, guided by the DESIGN outline
**Asks:** Does this section land its key point clearly? Does it use the right sources from RESEARCH? Does it connect to the section before and after?
**Produces:** Draft prose appended to `.draft/[post-slug].md`, plus notes on any deviations from the outline
**Before starting:** Read the active section's key point and source mapping from DESIGN — know what the section must do before writing a word

---

## Editor (CHECK)

**Focus:** Whether the post works as a whole and whether each section is as tight as it can be
**Asks (Editorial pass):** Does the argument hold? Are there gaps, unsupported claims, or sections that don't pull their weight? Does the tone match the audience? Does the opening earn the reader's attention? Does the close land?
**Asks (Polish pass):** Are transitions smooth? Is every sentence doing work? Are there weak openings or closings within sections? Is anything overwritten?
**Produces:** Editorial notes and inline edits in `.draft/[post-slug].md`
**Before starting:** Read the SHAPE brief — review against intent, not personal taste
